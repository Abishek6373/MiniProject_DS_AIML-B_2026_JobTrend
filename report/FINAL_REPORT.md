# Mini Project Report: JobTrend
## Data Analysis of Employment Market Trends

**Course:** Data Science Mini Project  
**Date:** May 2026  

---

### 1. Abstract
The JobTrend project analyzes the "1.3M LinkedIn Jobs & Skills (2024)" dataset to identify trending skills and market patterns. By focusing on tech-related roles, the project identifies a hybrid demand for technical proficiency (SQL, Python) and leadership capabilities. The findings align with SDG Goal 8 by providing transparency in the labor market.

### 2. Introduction
Understanding the job market is crucial for career planning. This project uses Labor Market Analytics to bridge the gap between education and employment requirements.

### 3. Methodology
The project followed a standard Data Science lifecycle:
- **Data Collection:** Sourced from Kaggle.
- **Data Cleaning:** Merged metadata with skill lists and filtered for tech keywords.
- **EDA:** Performed frequency analysis on skills and geographic distributions.
- **Visualization:** Created bar charts and count plots using Seaborn.

### 4. Data Analysis & Visualization
- **Skill Trends:** Top skills were extracted using Tokenization and Frequency Counting.
- **Geographic Insights:** Mapped job volume to specific cities.
- **Seniority Analysis:** Evaluated the ratio of Associate vs. Senior roles.

### 5. Results
- **Skills:** SQL and Project Management are top-tier requirements.
- **Growth Areas:** Data-driven roles show a high concentration in tech hubs.
- **Market State:** The market is currently "Senior-heavy," requiring specialized skills for entry.

### 6. Conclusion
The JobTrend project successfully mapped the 2024 employment landscape. Future work could involve time-series forecasting to predict the "next big skill" in AI/ML.
