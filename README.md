# JobTrend: Data Analysis of Employment Market Trends

## Abstract
The JobTrend project is a comprehensive data analysis initiative aimed at understanding the rapidly evolving employment landscape. In today's dynamic economy, students and professionals often face challenges in identifying which skills are most valued by employers. This project leverages the "1.3M LinkedIn Jobs & Skills (2024)" open dataset to identify trending skills, salary correlations across different roles, and geographic variations in job demand. By applying data science techniques such as data cleaning, exploratory data analysis (EDA), and data visualization, we aim to provide actionable insights that help individuals make informed career decisions. The project aligns with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 8 (SDG 8), promoting decent work and economic growth by bridging the information gap between job seekers and market requirements.

## Problem Statement
Understanding job market trends helps students and professionals make informed career decisions. The primary challenge is the overwhelming volume of job data and the high rate of skill obsolescence. This project seeks to analyze large-scale job posting datasets to identify which skills are currently in high demand and how they correlate with industry sectors and compensation.

## Dataset Source
- **Name:** 1.3M LinkedIn Jobs & Skills (2024)
- **Source:** Kaggle
- **URL:** [https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/asaniczka/1-3m-linkedin-jobs-and-skills-2024](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/asaniczka/1-3m-linkedin-jobs-and-skills-2024)

## Methodology / Workflow
1. **Problem Identification:** Defining the scope of skill trend analysis.
2. **Dataset Collection:** Sourcing job posting data from Kaggle.
3. **Data Cleaning / Preprocessing:** Handling missing values, tokenizing skills, and formatting dates.
4. **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA):** Identifying top skills, average salaries, and company distributions.
5. **Data Visualization:** Creating charts for skill demand, geographic heatmaps, and salary trends.
6. **Model Development (Optional):** Trend forecasting (if applicable).
7. **Result Interpretation:** Summarizing findings for career guidance.

## Tools Used
- **Language:** Python
- **Libraries:** Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn, Scikit-learn, NumPy
- **Environment:** Jupyter Notebooks

## Results / Findings
Our analysis of over 1.3 million LinkedIn job postings (2024) revealed the following key trends:
- **Top In-Demand Skills:** The market shows a strong preference for a blend of technical and soft skills. The top 15 skills include **Communication, Leadership, Project Management, SQL, Data Analysis, and Python**.
- **Seniority Trends:** A significant majority (~90% in our sample) of current job postings are targeted at **Mid-Senior level** professionals, indicating a competitive market for entry-level applicants.
- **Geographic Hubs:** Technology hiring remains concentrated in major urban centers, with **New York, San Francisco, and Austin** emerging as the top three hiring locations in the United States.
- **Work Type:** Despite the rise of remote work, **Onsite** remains the most frequently listed job type in the recent LinkedIn dataset sample.
